Wireframe Sites

Reddit

Reddit is trying to give you content based on popularity and your preferences. Reddit suffers from a clutter problem. Proper spacing, cleaner lines, better use of white space, and colors can help give a better sense of boundaries and natural grouping.

Quora

Quora tries to provide a Q&A site where reputation (aka upvote and downvote) drives the popularity and legitimacies of the answers. Quora's website is very clean and might not benefit much from a redesign unless users cry for a change or extension of its current raison d'etre.

Buzzfeed

Buzzfeed brings you the hightlights of entertaining topics. While buzzfeed makes great use of images to entice and entertain, it may benefit from de-emphasizing the images and creating more of a headings based list view. Having too many images can make the website can create too much visual cacaphony.
